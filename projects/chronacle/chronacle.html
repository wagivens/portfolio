Chronacle

Up until 2023, I had never owned a passport; I had never flown (especially internationally) despite having many places I wanted to visit. For a couple years, I used a spreadsheet in Notion to list destinations, sights, and interesting restaurants, but eventually, I decided that I wanted a better way to plan my future trips; I imagined others did too, especially given the boom in travel following the COVID-19 pandemic.

I saw that as an opportunity to grow and reinforce my design skills by creating a tool for myself and others to use. It is named Chronacle since it is meant to help you chronicle your journeys around the world.

Generative research using market data from Mintel showed me folks are taking fewer, shorter trips post-pandemic and only traveling to familiar places. Travelers are reluctant to visit new places but need helpful starting points when they do go somewhere new; they want to see as many options as possible for things to do.

People plan trips mere days or weeks before leaving, and use their preferred brands' sites (ex: Marriott Hotels) to book accommodations like hotels/flights/rental cars instead of using travel agency sites (ex: TripAdvisor).

As such, I decided that unlike the trip planning apps I tested when doing heuristic analyses, Chronacle would not handle things like flights, hotel reservations, car rentals, etc. Not only is the reduced scope better for engineering purposes, but users get a much more focused and predictable experience with fewer potential failure points.

I figured the focus for Chronacle should be on activities and experiences you might not reserve in advance, but still want to make plans for.

Chronacle's design system is being made with Storybook, allowing me to keep future designs consistent by using things like design tokens, as well as make development flows simple and seamless.
