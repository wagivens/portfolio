Overview

Problem: School children across the United States are attending classes online. Students, especially young ones, are showing declines in interest, focus, and academic performance; students need ways to stay motivated until in-person classes resume (and beyond). How might we motivate young children stay engaged with school during distanced learning?

The Solution

Books N Bucks is essentially a platform for teachers to incentivize students during distance learning. When students perform well (arriving on time, completing homework, helping classmates, etc), they are awarded points, which can then be used to purchase various items. The product owners had an existing site, but wanted us (my university course) to revamp it. My team focused on the Student Dashboard only.

Our product owners had some non-negotiable features: a store, a section for students to see their points, an attendance page, and a section for students to have a reward goal.

Crafting A Persona

We began our Design phase by brainstorming miniature personas for target users. We generally agreed the people using the dashboard would be grade-school students, many of whom are in need of positive reinforcement during distance learning.

Wireframes

We dot mapped our favorite ideas from everyone's sketches, and then we incorporated them into some early wireframes. Our designs focused on card-like elements and large content to appeal to young children without overwhelming or confusing them. We met the product owners' requirements, and we found ways to enhance what they wanted.

We believed:

  • The points and reward goal should be front-and-center, motivating students each time they log in.
  • Students should be able to see their points history and why they were given them (making them more likely to repeat those actions in the future).
  • Students should be able to save Store items and set them as their reward goal right from a wishlist.

For the Store page, I suggested using various colors and copy for the purchase state of an item to differentiate between lack of points and out-of-stock.

Testing Our Wireframes

We then tested our wireframes over Zoom with 3 students at an LAUSD school that was already using Books N Bucks. We noticed:

  • Students enjoyed seeing their points and reward goal on the Home page.
  • Being able to save Store items to a wishlist was preferable to searching for it again in the Store.
  • Iconography on the Attendance page was confusing.

Final Prototypes

Prototyping and putting together final designs was our next step. The product owners had their own color scheme of red, white, and black, so we adhered to those design guidelines. We decided to use Montserrat as our primary font because it is simple but slightly playful. We also added pagination to multiple pages to prevent overloading students with information and choices.

I was responsible for incorporating feedback into the Attendance page design. Since our testing participants told us the iconography was confusing, I decided we should switch to colored badge elements; coloring them helps convey status, but using words instead of icons makes it accessible for those who can't interpret the colors.

We tested the final prototypes as well with 3 more LAUSD students. We received no negative feedback, so we were confident that we addressed all of the concerns from our wireframe testing. From there, we moved on to developing the front and back ends of the dashboard.

Development

The front-end was a combination of React and SCSS, and we developed the front-end over the course of 2 weeks. As with the prototypes, I was mostly responsible for implementing the Attendance page. The back-end was done using Google Firebase and Google Cloud Functions, and was developed over the course of 2 weeks as well.
